Deviled Eggs

The original recipe called these "Million Dollar Deviled Eggs"... and honestly, the name isn't far off!

Please don't mind my somewhat terrible boiled egg peeling technique.

Prep Time:

20 minutes

Cook Time:

15 minutes

Total Time:

20 minutes

Servings:

24 eggs

Personal Enjoyment Rating:

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

These eggs are always a hit no matter what occasion I make them for-- and I have to say, I agree with the rave reviews!

Ingredients

  • 12 large eggs
  • ¼ cup (55 g) mayo
  • 1 Tablespoon butter (softened to room temperature)
  • 2 teaspoons yellow mustard
  • 2 teaspoons dijon mustard
  • 2 teaspoons sweet gherkin pickle juice
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • ⅛ teaspoon salt
  • ⅛ teaspoon pepper
  • dash Tabasco sauce (optional)
  • paprika for sprinkling
  • bacon pieces or bits (optional)

Instructions

  1. Place eggs in a large saucepan and cover with water.
  2. Transfer to stovetop over high heat until water begins to boil.
  3. Boil for one minute, cover with lid, and remove from heat. Allow to sit for 17 minutes and then drain and transfer to an ice bath.
  4. Peel eggs and set aside.
  5. Slice eggs in half lengthwise.
  6. Remove yolks and transfer to a medium-sized bowl.
  7. Add mayo, butter, mustards, pickle juice, sugar, salt, pepper, and Tabasco sauce. Use a fork to mash well.
  8. Spoon filling into each egg white. Sprinkle with paprika and bacon (if using) and serve.
  9. If desired, top with a small slice of sweet gherkin pickle, as seen in pictures.
