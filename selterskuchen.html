Selterskuchen (Seltzer Cake)

Who knew adding sparkling water to a cake could make such a big difference?

Sorry about the cracks in the cake, they don't quite make for a good picture... but it was delicious either way!

Prep Time:

25 minutes

Cook Time:

30 minutes

Total Time:

1 hour and 25 minutes total

Servings:

24 pieces

Personal Enjoyment Rating:

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

I ended up trying to add carbonated water to some other cakes too to see if it would have the same effect
after trying it with this recipe. I think that speaks for itself regarding how good I thought it was!

Ingredients

  • 4 eggs
  • 250 g sugar
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 150 ml of a neutral oil (sunflower oil, vegetable oil, etc.)
  • 175 ml sparkling water
  • 1 tbsp butter (for the pan)
  • powdered sugar, to taste (for topping)

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Grease a pan or line it with baking paper.
  3. For the batter, whisk together the eggs, sugar, vanilla extract, and oil. Mix the baking powder with the flour and add it to the egg and sugar mixture. Stir everything into a smooth batter. Finally, add the sparkling water and stir briefly.
  4. Pour the dough on the pan and spread it until smooth.
  5. Bake the cake in the oven for approximately 25-30 minutes.
  6. After baking, allow to cool completely for 30 minutes.
  7. Enjoy!


    8. Note: This cake stays fresh the longest when you leave it in the pan and put a lid on it. It can be stored at room temperature for approximately 3-4 days.
