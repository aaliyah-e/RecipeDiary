Loaded Mashed Potato Casserole

Delicious and cheesy goodness! This is the result of two recipes being mixed together.

When I say "loaded", I mean LOADED.

Prep Time:

15 minutes

Cook Time:

50 minutes

Total Time:

1 hour and 5 minutes

Servings:

10

Personal Enjoyment Rating:

★ ★ ★ ★ ★

I hate making these mashed potatoes but I love eating them, so it's worth the work.

Ingredients

  • 4 slices bacon or 1 small package bacon bits, or more to taste
  • 3 pounds potatoes, or more to taste, peeled and cut into chunks
  • ½ cup butter, cut into pieces
  • 1 (16 ounce) container sour cream
  • 8 ounces shredded Cheddar cheese
  • salt and ground black pepper to taste
  • 1 (8 ounce) package cream cheese, softened
  • ½ (6 ounce) can French fried onions

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 300 degrees F (150 degrees C). Grease a 1 1/2 quart casserole dish.
  2. If using bacon slices, place bacon in a large skillet and cook over medium-high heat, turning occasionally, until crisp and browned, about 10 minutes; drain on paper towels.
  3. Meanwhile, place potatoes into a large pot of lightly salted water and bring to a boil; reduce heat to medium-low and simmer until tender, about 20 minutes. Drain well and return potatoes to the pot.
  4. Mash butter into the potatoes using a potato masher or electric hand mixer until well combined. Add sour cream and continue to mash or beat until combined. Crumble bacon into potato mixture; stir in Cheddar cheese and chives. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Combine mashed potatoes, cream cheese, onion, eggs, flour, salt and black pepper in an electric mixer. Beat 2 to 3 minutes at medium speed. Pour into prepared casserole dish. Spread fried onions evenly over the top.
  6. Bake uncovered for 30 to 35 minutes.
  7. Enjoy!
